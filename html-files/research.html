Research Projects

  1. Numerical stabilization of the Navier-Stokes-Boussinesq system

    We study the numerical stabilization of two-dimensional Navier-Stokes-Boussinesq equations. The stabilization is achieved by boundary feedback controls of finite dimension applied on the velocity and temperature, and localized on parts of domain boundary. We consider a stationary solution of the Boussinesq system which is linearly unstable. The linearized system around this unstable stationary solution is used to construct a feedback law. For that, we determine the projected linearized system onto an invariant subspace (including the unstable subspace), and we determine a feedback control law stabilizing this projected system, by solving a Ricatti equation, of small dimension, associated with this system. The linear feedback law, stabilizing the projected system, is applied to the non-linear model to study its ability to locally stabilize the flow and its temperature

    In collaboration with: Prof. Praveen Chandrashekar, Prof. Mythily Ramaswamy, Prof. Jean-Pierre Raymond
    Status: To be submitted, 2020

  2. Adaptive finite element methods for generalized Nash equilibrium problems

    We are developing an a posteriori error analysis of adaptive finite element approximations of generalized Nash equilibrium problem with pointwise control and state constraints. Moreau-Yosida-based regularization of the state constrained problem is considered. The error analysis is based on a residual-type a posteriori error estimator that consists of edge and element residuals. The reliability and efficiency of the error estimator will be estimated and a bulk criterion for mesh refinement that also takes into account data oscillations and is realized by a greedy algorithm will be provided. Finally, we plan to conduct numerical tests for illustration of the convergence of the adaptive finite element method.

    In collaboration with: Prof. Michael Hintermüller, Dr. Caroline Löbhard
    Status: Ongoing

  3. Discontinuous finite volume methods for optimal control problems (Ph.D. Thesis)

    In my Ph.D. work, we have developed and analyzed a robust numerical scheme called discontinuous finite volume method (DFVM) for the approximation of distributed optimal control problems subject to pointwise control constraints. The DFVM is a combination of finite volume element method and discontinuous Galerkin method and retain desirable properties of both methods such as mass conservation, local mesh adaptivity, element-wise conservative, allow different degree polynomials in different elements and easily handle the boundary conditions. We consider semilinear elliptic, semilinear parabolic, semilinear hyperbolic and Brinkman optimal control problems. The Brinkman equations describe the motion of an incompressible viscous fluid within an array of porous particles, and according to the flow regime characterized by the ratio between permeability and viscosity, it can represent both the Darcy and Stokes limits. We have derived optimal order a priori error estimtes in suitable natural norms and conducted numerical experiments to illustrate the performance of the proposed scheme and to confirm the predicted accuracy of the theoretical convergence rates.

    Status: Completed, 2017
